# Code of conduct

## Our standard

We want project spaces to be technically rigorous, welcoming, and safe. Be
respectful, assume good faith while asking for evidence, give actionable
feedback, and distinguish disagreement about an idea from judgment of a person.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, threats, discriminatory language,
sexualized attention, sustained disruption, deliberate disclosure of private
information, or retaliation against someone who reports a concern.

## Scope

This policy applies in the repository and in public spaces where someone is
representing the project, including issues, pull requests, discussions, release
channels, and project events.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions, lock discussions, issue warnings,
temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban participants when needed
to protect the community. Enforcement should be proportionate, documented
privately where possible, and avoid exposing a reporter or affected person.

For an ordinary technical disagreement, use the relevant issue or pull request.
For sensitive conduct concerns, contact the maintainer privately rather than
opening a public issue. The public repository will identify the private contact
route before accepting outside participation.
